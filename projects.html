Off Season is a nostalgic 8-bit puzzle game full of exploration and challenge. Programmed in C, Off Season is for the GameBoy Advance platform. I built the Off Season world from the ground up, doing everything from character design, visual design, and game design to in-game physics and gameplay. You can play it with a GameBoy Advance emulator like VisualBoy Advance, Boycott Advance, or OpenEmu. Off Season was inspired in part by Legend of Zelda Oracle of Seasons and Fez.

Favorite part of the project: figuring out how 8-bit art and low-level C fit into the big picture.

To see the project, check out edwinchoate.com/offseason

Greenlight.space is an IoT web app that brings all the functionality of a physical traffic light to your pocket. I worked with electrical engineering student George Tzintzarov to develop this project for fun. I carried out all of the visual design, experience design, and front-end development for the project. Written in Python, Greenlight.space is a Flask micro app that is deployed to Heroku. On the server side, the physical traffic light is controlled by scripts residing on a Raspberry Pi 2 (as a result, the site does have some downtime).

Favorite part of the project: connecting UX and IoT.

See the project live at greenlight.space

Campus Outreach Atlanta serves hundreds of students at six college campuses in the metro Atlanta area. As lead designer and developer, I met with the Campus Outreach Staff Team to design the site according to their needs. Using Adobe Muse, I created the entire desktop site and the majority of the mobile site. I employed user-centered design principles in assessing critical requirements such as the site's donations page. I worked with the team's Media Developer to populate the site with compelling images and videos of current students.

Favorite part of the project: working with the client to build something they love.

See the finished product at coatlanta.org

TwitterTicker is a visualization that juxtaposes stock and Twitter data to help better understand how social media can play a role in the stock market. I built TwitterTicker as part of a team project for the Introduction to Information Visualization (CS 4460) course at Georgia Tech. As the lead front-end developer, I carried out the visual design, widget design, and interaction design. I also built the project's Bootstrap site and implemented two of the three views: the "Stock Timeline View" and "Magnet View". The TwitterTicker front-end utilizes D3 and jQuery for interaction.

Favorite part of the project: working with back-end developers to bring large datasets to life (~30,000 tweets per company).

Give the project a try at edwinchoate.com/TwitterTicker

audio.js

This just-for-fun project is a fork of Anthony Kolber's HTML5 music player, audio.js. In designing an audio player for my site, I wanted to focus on modernism and minimalism. I modified the original by bringing in flat and responsive design. This version of audio.js can be seen in action on my music page.

Favorite part of the project: the combination of aesthetics, functionality, and music.
